What Are Loading Metres?

A loading metre (LDM) — also written as loading meter or "laadmeter" — is the standard unit used across the UK and Europe to measure the floor space a consignment occupies in a road freight trailer. One loading metre represents a strip of trailer floor that is 1 metre long and the full width of the trailer (typically 2.4 metres wide).

Unlike CBM (cubic metres), which measures total volume, LDM measures only floor area. This matters because road freight is priced by floor space: a tall, light pallet takes up the same LDM as a short, heavy one. Understanding LDM lets you compare quotes, plan loads, and avoid paying for space you don't use.

LDM is used by most European road freight carriers and groupage operators. If you're booking a part-load or groupage service between the UK and Europe, you'll almost certainly be quoted per LDM.

The Loading Metres Formula

LDM = (Length × Width × Quantity) ÷ 2.4
— Where Length and Width are in metres, 2.4m = standard trailer width

If stackable:
LDM = (Length × Width × Quantity) ÷ 2.4 ÷ Stack Factor
— Stack Factor is 2 (double stack) or 3 (triple stack)

UK Standard pallets (1200 × 1000 mm) take up more floor space than Euro pallets (1200 × 800 mm). A full artic can carry 33 Euro pallets floor-only, but only around 26 UK Standard pallets in the same space.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate loading metres?
Loading metres are calculated using: LDM = (Length × Width × Quantity) ÷ 2.4, where Length and Width are in metres, and 2.4m is the standard trailer width. For a single Euro pallet (1.2m × 0.8m): LDM = (1.2 × 0.8 × 1) ÷ 2.4 = 0.4 LDM. If goods are stackable and double-stacked, divide the result by the stacking factor (2 or 3).
What is 1 LDM?
One loading metre (1 LDM) represents a strip of trailer floor that is exactly 1 metre long and 2.4 metres wide — the full internal width of a standard European trailer. It's the standard unit used by road freight carriers across the UK and EU for quoting and comparing part-load (groupage) shipments. To put it in context: 2.5 Euro pallets side-by-side equal approximately 1 LDM.
How many Euro pallets fit in a 13.6m trailer?
A standard 13.6m articulated trailer can carry 33 Euro pallets (1200 × 800mm) in a single floor layer. Loaded in a block (landscape) configuration, it fits 26 pallets. If double-stacked, up to 66 Euro pallets can be carried, subject to height restrictions (maximum internal height is typically 2.7m) and weight limits (maximum payload ~24,000 kg).
What is the difference between LDM and CBM?
LDM (Loading Metres) measures the floor space a consignment occupies in a trailer — used primarily for European road freight and groupage services. CBM (Cubic Metres) measures the total volume of a shipment, and is used more widely in air freight and sea freight. For road freight across the UK and EU, LDM is the industry-standard pricing unit. CBM is more relevant when booking container or airfreight shipments.
